How to make the best Bison Meatballs of all time!

Inspired by his tales and armed with newfound knowledge, I set out to craft my own bison meatball recipe. I wanted to blend the essence of traditional flavors with a touch of modern innovation, staying true to my culinary roots.

The resulting recipe was a harmonious symphony of flavors, a fusion of culinary traditions and contemporary techniques. And thus, my culinary journey continued, fueled by the magic of unexpected encounters and the joy of creating delightful dishes that would leave a lasting mark on all who tasted them.


Ingredients required:

  • 1 lb of ground bison meat
  • 1/2 cup of breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup of grated parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (for a little optional heat)
  • Olive oil (for frying, use your judgement)
    •

    Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground bison meat, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped onion, minced garlic, milk, egg, salt, black pepper, dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes (if using). Mix everything together until well combined.
  • Take small portions of the mixture and roll them into meatballs. The size is up to your preference, but typically, they are about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter.
  • Heat a skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat and add a little olive oil to coat the bottom of the pan.
  • Place the meatballs in the hot skillet, making sure not to overcrowd them. You may need to cook them in batches if your pan is not large enough.
  • Brown the meatballs on all sides, turning them as needed. This should take about 4-5 minutes.
  • Once the meatballs are nicely browned, transfer them to the prepared baking sheet.
  • Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for about 10-15 minutes or until they are cooked through.
  • Bison meat is lean, so be careful not to overcook them to prevent them from becoming dry.
  • Serve the bison meatballs with your favorite sauce or pasta, or use them as appetizers.
